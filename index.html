How GridPool works

GridPool does not try to reproduce traditional pool accounting. Instead, it lets miners compete for slots in the next coinbase payout list by submitting high-difficulty shares that any peer can verify.

01

Build your own block

Your mining stack constructs a Bitcoin block template locally. The block pays slot 0 to the block finder and shares the rest across the current Winners List.

02

Submit high-difficulty proofs

When your miner finds a share strong enough to compete for the on-deck list, your node relays the header, coinbase, and Merkle proof needed for trustless verification.

03

Rank by verified work

Peers verify the proof-of-work and attribute the share from the slot-0 coinbase output. Identities do not vote, so Sybil accounts do not create extra payout weight.

04

Pay from the coinbase

When a GridPool miner finds a real Bitcoin block, payouts happen directly from that block's coinbase transaction. There is no central wallet waiting to distribute funds later.

Different tradeoffs

Not a normal pool. Not pure solo.

Compared with classic pools

GridPool does not collect block rewards into a pool wallet, does not decide your transaction template, and does not pay from a centralized ledger. The tradeoff is higher variance than FPPS or PPLNS.

Compared with sharechains

GridPool avoids maintaining a second blockchain. There is no sharechain history to reorg, privately mine, or 51 percent attack as a separate consensus object.

Compared with solo mining

GridPool keeps the local-control feel of solo mining but shares the block subsidy across recent high-difficulty contributors, reducing payout variance by up to roughly 300x.

Public beta connection guide

Connect through DATUM, not raw Stratum V1.

GridPool is built for sovereign miners who can construct their own block templates. ASICs should connect to your DATUM gateway. DATUM then talks to a GridPool Boot node.

1. Run DATUM

Configure DATUM with your payout address and a Bitcoin node capable of building templates.

2. Open a node UI

Use the Connect DATUM panel on main.gridpool.net or test.gridpool.net for the current Boot endpoint details.

3. Point ASICs at DATUM

Your miner talks Stratum to DATUM. Do not point raw Stratum V1 directly at this website or at the Boot WebUI.

4. Verify your address

Once shares arrive, the node UI should show your local hashrate and whether your address is currently on the payout lists.

Integrator note

Hydrapool support is planned. Other sovereign Stratum servers such as CK Pool, Public Pool, Stratum V2 implementations, and FutureBit-style stacks can integrate through the HTTP share-submission API.
